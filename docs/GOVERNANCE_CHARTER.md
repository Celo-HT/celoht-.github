# CeloHT Governance Charter

_Last updated: July 20, 2026_

## Overview

This Governance Charter defines the principles, structures, and processes that guide decision-making within CeloHT.

CeloHT is a community-governed open-source initiative. The purpose of governance is to ensure transparency, participation, accountability, and long-term sustainability.

---

# Governance Principles

CeloHT governance is based on:

- Transparency
- Community participation
- Open collaboration
- Accountability
- Inclusiveness
- Sustainability
- Respectful dialogue

---

# Community Governance Model

CeloHT follows a community-driven governance model.

Important decisions are discussed openly with contributors, community members, and relevant stakeholders.

Governance authority comes from transparent community processes rather than individual control.

---

# Governance Participants

The CeloHT community may include:

## Community Members

Individuals who participate in discussions, programs, education, or community activities.

---

## Contributors

People who contribute through:

- Code
- Documentation
- Design
- Research
- Translation
- Community support

---

## Maintainers

Trusted contributors responsible for:

- Reviewing contributions
- Maintaining repositories
- Improving project quality
- Supporting technical operations

---

## Program Contributors

Individuals supporting specific initiatives such as:

- Education
- Agent Network
- Reforestation
- Community programs

---

# Decision-Making Process

## Small Changes

Examples:

- Documentation improvements
- Minor fixes
- Content updates

These may be handled through normal contribution workflows.

---

## Major Decisions

Examples:

- New programs
- Major technical changes
- Strategic partnerships
- Governance changes

These should involve:

1. Community discussion
2. Proposal review
3. Feedback collection
4. Final decision through the appropriate governance process

---

# Governance Proposals

A governance proposal should include:

- Title
- Background
- Objectives
- Expected impact
- Required resources
- Risks
- Community discussion period

---

# Voting and Consensus

CeloHT prioritizes consensus and community discussion.

When formal voting is required, the process should be transparent and documented.

Voting mechanisms may evolve as the community grows.

---

# Conflict Resolution

Disagreements should be addressed through:

1. Respectful discussion
2. Mediation when necessary
3. Community guidance
4. Application of relevant policies

All participants are expected to follow the Code of Conduct.

---

# Transparency

Governance information may include:

- Proposals
- Discussions
- Decisions
- Roadmap updates
- Program reports

Public documentation helps maintain trust.

---

# Funding and Governance Independence

Financial support does not grant control over CeloHT governance.

Sponsors, donors, or partners cannot override community decisions through financial contributions.

---

# Leadership and Roles

Roles within CeloHT exist to support the community.

Leadership responsibilities are based on contribution, trust, and accountability.

No role represents individual ownership of the community.

---

# Updates to Governance

This Governance Charter may be updated through the community governance process.

Changes should be documented and communicated publicly.

---

# Community Commitment

Every participant contributes to building a transparent, inclusive, and sustainable ecosystem.

Through collaboration and shared responsibility, CeloHT continues to grow as a community-driven initiative.
