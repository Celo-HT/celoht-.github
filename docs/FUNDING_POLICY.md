# Funding Policy

_Last updated: July 20, 2026_

## Overview

CeloHT is a community-governed open-source initiative.

Financial support helps expand education, digital inclusion, environmental sustainability, open-source development, and community programs.

This document explains the principles that guide how CeloHT accepts, manages, and uses funding.

---

# Funding Principles

All funding should support the mission of CeloHT while preserving:

- Community governance
- Transparency
- Accountability
- Independence
- Sustainability
- Ethical collaboration

Funding must never compromise the values or long-term goals of the community.

---

# Accepted Sources of Funding

CeloHT may receive support through:

- Grants
- Donations
- Sponsorships
- Community contributions
- Corporate social responsibility (CSR) programs
- Educational partnerships
- Technical assistance
- Infrastructure support
- In-kind contributions

Each funding opportunity is evaluated individually.

---

# Funding Restrictions

CeloHT may decline funding that:

- Conflicts with the organization's mission.
- Requires unethical conditions.
- Reduces community independence.
- Creates conflicts of interest.
- Violates applicable laws or regulations.
- Damages the reputation of the community.

Acceptance of funding does not imply endorsement of a donor's products, services, or views.

---

# Use of Funds

Funding may be used to support activities such as:

- Community education
- Educational materials
- Workshops and events
- Volunteer support
- Open-source software development
- Documentation
- Infrastructure and hosting
- Environmental initiatives
- Community outreach
- Research and innovation

---

# Financial Transparency

CeloHT is committed to responsible financial management.

When appropriate, the community may publish reports summarizing:

- Funding sources
- Program expenditures
- Community initiatives
- Impact achieved

Some information may remain confidential when required by law, contractual obligations, or privacy considerations.

---

# Community Governance

Funding decisions should follow the governance process established by CeloHT.

No individual, sponsor, donor, or partner may override community governance through financial contributions.

---

# Sponsorship Recognition

Sponsors may be acknowledged through:

- Official documentation
- Community reports
- Project websites
- Events
- Public appreciation

Recognition does not imply endorsement or decision-making authority.

---

# Conflicts of Interest

Community members involved in funding decisions should disclose any actual or potential conflicts of interest.

Where appropriate, affected individuals should abstain from related decisions.

---

# Compliance

CeloHT seeks to comply with applicable laws and regulations governing fundraising, financial management, and nonprofit activities in the jurisdictions where it operates.

---

# Policy Updates

This Funding Policy may be updated through the community governance process.

The latest published version replaces previous versions.

---

# Contact

Questions regarding funding, sponsorships, grants, or donations may be directed through the official CeloHT communication channels.
